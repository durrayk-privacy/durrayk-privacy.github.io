EU AI Act risk tiers

Unacceptable

Social scoring · real-time biometric surveillance

Blocked from deployment regardless of vendor pressure or business case.

High Risk

AI grading · behavioral prediction · automated admissions

Full DPIA required · human oversight documented · annual review mandated.

Limited Risk

AI chatbots · content recommendation engines

Transparency notice required · users informed · logged in AI inventory.

Minimal Risk

Spell check · scheduling · non-personalized analytics

Noted in AI inventory · no additional controls required.

DPIA / PIA framework

AI systems receive a dedicated template covering training data, automated decisions, bias risk, data minimisation, and third-party model risk — reviewed by me as data specialist for gaps.

01

Scoping

Identify the system. Define data flows, data subjects, and processing purpose.

02

Necessity & proportionality

Assess whether processing is necessary and proportionate. Document legal basis.

03

Risk identification

Map risks: unauthorized access, re-identification, profiling, cross-border transfer, retention overage.

04

Risk rating

Likelihood (1–3) × Impact (1–3) = Risk Score. Flag anything above threshold for immediate escalation.

05

Controls & mitigation

For each risk: define control owner, control type, target remediation date.

06

DPO / Legal review

Submit for sign-off. Flag high-risk findings to leadership.

07

Sign-off & documentation

Record outcome. Archive in evidence library. Schedule next review date.
